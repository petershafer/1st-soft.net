Before you read on...

· Sinfulness rates how "unhealthy" a drink is --not in caffeine, but in sugar, fat, and calorie content.
· Caffeine and sinfulness ratings are relative to the size of the drink. Typically, a small drink is one shot each of espresso and syrup, a medium drink is two, and a large drink is three. For generality's sake, the preparations and ratings are based on medium sizes.

Coffee Drink Listing

Boiler Maker (A.K.A. "Red Eye")
Ingredients: Espresso, coffee.
Preparation: Brew and pour shot(s) of espresso, add coffee, fill cup.
Equipment: Espresso machine, coffee brewer.
Caffeine Rating: 4/5
Sinfulness: 1/5

Brevé
Ingredients: Espresso, steamed cream.
Preparation: Brew shot(s) of espresso, steam cream, fill cup. Foam optional.
Equipment: Espresso machine.
Caffeine Rating: 3/5
Sinfulness: 5/5

Café Americano
Ingredients: Espresso, hot water.
Preparation: Brew and pour shot(s) of espresso, add very hot water, fill cup.
Equipment: Espresso machine.
Caffeine rating: 4/5
Sinfulness: 1/5

Café Au Lait
Ingredients: Coffee and steamed milk.
Preparation: Fill cup halfway with brewed coffee, then halfway with steamed milk.
Equipment: Coffee brewer, steam wand.
Caffeine rating: 1/5
Sinfulness: 2/5

Caramel Macchiato
Ingredients: Espresso, steamed milk, caramel, and vanilla.
Preparation: Pour 2 shots of caramel, 1 shot vanilla syrup, add steamed milk, pour brewed shot(s) of espresso through top of milk foam.
Equipment: Espresso machine.
Caffeine Rating: 3/5
Sinfulness: 4/5

Caramel Mocha
Ingredients: Espresso, steamed milk, chocolate, and caramel.
Preparation: Pour 2 shots caramel, 1 shot chocolate, brewed shot(s) of espresso, add steamed milk, and typically top with whipped cream and chocolate and caramel drizzle.
Equipment: Espresso machine.
Caffeine Rating: 3/5
Sinfulness: 5/5

Cappuccino
Ingredients: Espresso, steamed milk.
Preparation: Pour brewed shot(s) of espresso, steam milk and pull away so that the steam bubbles and froths the milk, pour and strain with spoon until cup is 2/3 full, and top with plenty of foam.
Equipment: Espresso machine.
Caffeine Rating: 3/5
Sinfulness: 2/5 (3/5 if you add a flavor, like French Vanilla or English Toffee.)

Espresso
Ingredients: Espresso.
Preparation: Pour brewed shot(s) of espresso into small cup.
Equipment: Espresso machine.
Caffeine Rating: 5/5
Sinfulness: 1/5

Latté
Ingredients: Espresso, steamed milk.
Preparation: Pour brewed shot(s) of espresso, add steamed milk, and a small layer of foam.
Equipment: Espresso machine.
Caffeine Rating: 3/5
Sinfulness: 2/5 (3/5 if you add a flavor, like Hazelnut or Peppermint.)

Mocha
Ingredients: Espresso, steamed milk, dark or milk chocolate.
Preparation: Pour 2 shots of dark or milk chocolate, brew and pour shot(s) of espresso, add steamed milk, and typically add whipped cream and chocolate drizzle.
Equipment: Espresso machine.
Caffeine Rating: 3/5
Sinfulness: 4/5

White Mocha
Ingredients: Espresso, steamed milk, white chocolate.
Preparation: Pour 2 shots of white chocolate, brew and pour shot(s) of espresso, add steamed milk, and typically add whipped cream.
Equipment: Espresso machine.
Caffeine Rating: 3/5
Sinfulness: 4/5

More Options

· Skim, soy, and lactaid milk are usually available for drinks, as well as sugar-free flavors.
· Decaffeinated versions are always available, even for espresso drinks!
· You can ice any drink as well, or have them made into a smoothie using ice-cream mix as a substitute for milk/cream.

Tips

· When steaming milk, the ideal end temperature is around 120 degrees Fahrenheit. Use a coffee thermometer if you do not have an automatic steamer. If the milk gets too hot it will scald and taste terrible.
· Espresso (and all coffee, for that matter) flavor is best when you grind the beans just before brewing! Try it once, and you'll never go back.
· Likewise, don't settle for sub-par additives, like powdered chocolate for mochas.
· If you want to make espresso at home, invest in a quality machine. You won't regret it.
· Special drinks listed at coffeehouses can be limitless, since new combination drinks are often invented on the spot. Every café has their own eclectic drinks, but this list has all the originals.
· Why not experiment yourself, once you've tried these out?

Italian Coffee Slang Dictionary

Con Panna: Requests whipped cream with your drink.
Crema: The gold foam that tops espresso.
Lungo: Espresso shot made with extra hot water.
Macchiato: Espresso with a dollop of milk foam on top.
Ristretto: Espresso shot made with minimal hot water.
Romano: Espresso with a slice of lemon on the side.

American Coffee Slang Dictionary

Café Latté: Identical to the latté, which is espresso and steamed milk (see drink section).
Dry Cappuccino: A cappuccino that is mostly foam.
Double: Two shots of espresso.
Fake Latté/Cappuccino: Decaf latté or cappuccino (credit to customer Dave).
Lattéccino: Use to request a drink with a milk foam level between that of a latté and cappuccino.
Skinny Latté/Cappuccino: A cappuccino or latté made with skim or fat-free milk.
Tall: Can mean a medium or small size drink, depending on the café.
Triple: Three shots of espresso.
Whipless: Requests that whipped cream be left off of your drink.
